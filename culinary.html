Popular Recipes

  • Dark Chocolate Brownies

    Ingredients:
    • 100 g dark chocolate
    • ½ cup butter
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 2 eggs
    • ½ cup flour
    • 1 tsp vanilla
    Method:
    1. Melt dark chocolate and butter together.
    2. Mix in sugar, eggs, and vanilla.
    3. Fold in flour gently.
    4. Bake at 180°C for 20–25 minutes.

  • Chocolate Mousse

    Ingredients:
    • 150 g dark chocolate
    • 1 cup whipped cream
    • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
    Method:
    1. Melt chocolate and cool slightly.
    2. Fold gently into whipped cream.
    3. Pour into glasses.
    4. Chill for 2–3 hours.

  • Hot Dark Chocolate Drink

    Ingredients:
    • 1 cup milk
    • 50 g dark chocolate
    • 1 tsp cocoa powder (optional)
    • Sugar to taste
    Method:
    1. Heat milk (do not boil).
    2. Add chopped chocolate and stir.
    3. Add cocoa powder and sugar.
    4. Serve hot.

Tasting Guide

Look

  • Observe the color — deep brown to almost black.
  • A glossy shine indicates proper tempering.
  • Avoid bars with white streaks (chocolate bloom).

Smell

  • Break a small piece and smell it.
  • Notice aromas like fruity, nutty, earthy, floral, or spicy notes.
  • A strong cocoa aroma usually means high quality.

Snap

  • Break a piece in half.
  • Good dark chocolate makes a clean, sharp “snap.”
  • A dull break may indicate poor tempering.

Taste

  • Let it melt slowly on your tongue.
  • Identify flavor stages: initial taste, middle notes, and aftertaste.
  • Look for balance between bitterness, sweetness, and acidity.

Texture & Finish

  • It should feel smooth and not grainy.
  • Notice how long the flavor lingers (longer is usually better).

Tip: Taste chocolates with different cocoa percentages (60%, 70%, 85%) to compare intensity and flavor complexity.
